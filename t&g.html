Energy-Saving Tips & Guides

Unplug Idle Electronics

Devices like TVs, chargers, and coffee makers still consume "vampire energy" even when turned off. Unplug them when not in use to save up to 10% on your electricity bill.

Switch to LED Lighting

LED bulbs use up to 85% less energy than traditional incandescent bulbs and last much longer. It's a simple change that makes a big impact.

Use Smart Power Strips

These power strips can automatically shut off power to devices that aren't in use, helping you eliminate vampire energy without having to unplug everything.

Manage Your Thermostat

Set your thermostat a few degrees higher in the summer and a few degrees lower in the winter to significantly reduce heating and cooling costs. A smart thermostat can automate this for you.

Seal Air Leaks

Check for drafts around windows and doors and seal them with caulk or weatherstripping. This prevents conditioned air from escaping and makes your HVAC system work more efficiently.

Run Appliances at Off-Peak Hours

If your utility offers time-of-use pricing, running large appliances like your washing machine or dishwasher at night can save you money when energy is cheaper.
